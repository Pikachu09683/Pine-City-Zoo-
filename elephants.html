ELEPHANTS

Elephants are the largest land animals, characterized by their massive size, large ears, and long trunks .

They are found in Africa and Asia, living in diverse habitats like savannas, grasslands, and forests.

Their trunk is incredibly versatile, used for communication, handling objects, and even breathing when submerged. Elephants face threats like poaching and habitat loss, and efforts are underway to protect them.

frican elephants are the largest land animals, reaching shoulder heights of 3-4 meters (9.8-13.1 ft) and weighing between 4,000 and 6,300 kg (2 and 7 tons). Asian elephants are smaller, with shoulder heights between 2 and 3.5 meters (6.6 and 11.5 ft) and weights between 3,000 and 5,000 kg (2 and 5 tons).
